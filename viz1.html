This 4-panel shows the ICE (Internal Combustion Engine), MG1 (Small motor), MG2 (Large transaxle motor), and MGR (rear motor).

  • ICE spins between 0 and 5000rpm
  • MG1 spins between -7500 and 10000rpm. This motor does the most generation and conversion of energy.
  • MG2 spins between 0 and 5000rpm. This matches the power band of the ICE. MG2
  • MGR has no data. This makes sense because the Prius is FWD only.



Overlaying and highlighting the ICE, we can see a very even power band over time. The weekends were I do not drive are apparent from the gaps.



Overlaying and highlighting MG1, we see that this motor does the most conversion of energy by spinning backwards and forwards. This process charges the battery, provides regenative braking, and spins the fastest. The size of the motor is also the smallest, which allows it to reach 10krpm.



Overlaying and highlighting MG2, we see what we expect. This large motor provides propulsion and matches the power band of the ICE.
